# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members of Team 3 pledge to make participation in our project a respectful, professional, and enriching experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

Team members are expected to:

- ✅ **Communicate Respectfully** - Use welcoming and inclusive language
- ✅ **Show Empathy** - Be considerate of differing viewpoints and experiences
- ✅ **Accept Feedback Gracefully** - Welcome constructive criticism
- ✅ **Focus on Team Success** - Prioritize what's best for the team and project
- ✅ **Take Responsibility** - Own your commitments and acknowledge mistakes
- ✅ **Help Others Learn** - Share knowledge and teach one another
- ✅ **Participate Actively** - Attend meetings and contribute to discussions
- ✅ **Meet Deadlines** - Complete assigned tasks on time or communicate early if help is needed
- ✅ **Follow BYU-Idaho Honor Code** - Uphold the standards of the institution

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are unacceptable:

- ❌ Harassment, discrimination, or disrespectful comments
- ❌ Personal attacks or insults
- ❌ Trolling or deliberately inflammatory comments
- ❌ Plagiarism or dishonest academic conduct
- ❌ Sharing others' private information without permission
- ❌ Consistently missing deadlines without communication
- ❌ Failing to participate in team activities
- ❌ Any other conduct that would be considered unprofessional

## Team Collaboration Guidelines

### Communication

- Respond to team messages within 24-48 hours
- Use appropriate channels for different types of communication
- Be clear and concise in written communication
- Assume positive intent in others' messages

### Meetings

- Attend all scheduled team meetings
- Arrive on time and prepared
- Participate actively and contribute ideas
- Respect others' speaking time
- Stay focused on meeting objectives

### Code Reviews

- Provide constructive, actionable feedback
- Focus on the code, not the person
- Explain the reasoning behind suggestions
- Accept that there may be multiple valid solutions
- Thank reviewers for their time and feedback

### Conflict Resolution

If conflicts arise:

1. **Direct Discussion** - Try to resolve the issue directly with the involved party
2. **Team Leader Mediation** - Bring the issue to the current week's team leader
3. **Instructor Involvement** - If unresolved, consult with the course instructor
4. **Document Issues** - Keep records of persistent problems

## Responsibilities

### Team Leaders

The rotating team leader is responsible for:

- Facilitating productive meetings
- Ensuring everyone has a voice
- Mediating conflicts when they arise
- Communicating with the instructor when necessary
- Modeling professional behavior

### All Team Members

Every team member is responsible for:

- Upholding this Code of Conduct
- Reporting violations to the team leader or instructor
- Supporting a positive team environment
- Holding themselves accountable

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- Team meetings (in-person and virtual)
- Project repository and pull requests
- Team communication channels
- Code reviews and discussions
- Any team-related activities

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. Document the incident with dates and details
2. Report to the current team leader
3. If the leader is involved, report to another trusted team member or the instructor
4. All reports will be handled with discretion

### Consequences

Violations may result in:

1. **First Violation** - Private discussion and warning
2. **Second Violation** - Team meeting to address the issue
3. **Persistent Violations** - Involvement of course instructor
4. **Severe Violations** - Reporting to academic department per BYU-Idaho policies

## Academic Integrity

This project adheres to BYU-Idaho's Academic Honesty Policy:

- All work submitted must be our own or properly attributed
- Collaboration within the team is encouraged
- External resources must be cited appropriately
- AI assistance (like GitHub Copilot) should be used ethically and disclosed when significant

## BYU-Idaho Honor Code

All team members agree to live by the [BYU-Idaho Honor Code](https://www.byui.edu/student-honor-office/ces-honor-code), which includes:

- Being honest in all dealings
- Living a chaste and virtuous life
- Abstaining from substance abuse
- Using clean language
- Respecting others
- Upholding academic integrity

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is adapted from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/), version 2.1, and customized for our academic team environment.

## Questions or Concerns

If you have questions about this Code of Conduct, please:

- Ask during a team meeting
- Contact the current team leader
- Reach out to the course instructor
